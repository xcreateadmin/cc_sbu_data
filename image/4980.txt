This image is a representation of the element titanium. Titanium is a chemical element with the atomic number 22 and the symbol Ti. It is a transition metal known for its high strength and low density. It is commonly used in the manufacturing of aircraft, spacecraft, and other high-performance equipment due to its high strength to weight ratio. Titanium is also used in the production of medical implants such as hip replacements due to its biocompatibility with human tissue. It has a melting point of 1668°C and a boiling point of 3028°C, is highly resistant to corrosion, and is often used in marine and industrial environments, as well as in the production of jewelry and other decorative items.

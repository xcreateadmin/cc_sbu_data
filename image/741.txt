Constellation Leo is one of the twelve zodiac constellations in the night sky. It is named after the lion in Greek mythology and located in the northern hemisphere. Leo is easily recognizable by its seven bright stars, which form the shape of a lion's head and mane. The brightest star in Leo is Regulus at the base of the lion's tail. Leo covers an area of about 900 square degrees and is bordered by Cancer to the east, Hydra to the south, and Virgo to the west. It is visible in the northern hemisphere from around July to October and is best viewed in the early evening during the summer months.
